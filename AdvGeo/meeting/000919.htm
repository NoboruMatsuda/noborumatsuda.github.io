Noboru Matsuda

Insight and discovery

  • Simon et al, "Science Discovery" is a good place to start.
  • Insight needs a surprising
    • A surprising makes you change a focus of attention.
    • To be surprised, one needs to be knowledgeable.  Otherwise, how could he find something neat?
    • Here is a interesting number "10" which is an average number of years you need to be an expert in a certain domain.
  • Insight also needs a skill of "testing."
    • Without a bunch of trials, no insight never occur.
  • We need a theory of the process of insight.
  • Insight can be trainable.
    • Remember an experiment conducted by Simon et al to teach students how to explore a chemical domain in the lab. 
    •  They could actually find some interesting facts.
  • Two different objects which are involved in the process of discovery:
    • Observable objects
    • mental / conceptual objects.
    • To see "observable objects" is easy, to know "mental" objects is hard.
    • Making mental object is similar to AL construction.
