GRAMY meeting minutes

Noboru Matsuda

Starting point

We haven't known the relationship between general construction and old GRAMY's specialized construction.

Wish to know it, because that would convince us the completeness of GRAMY as well as an educational value of GRAMY. 

General construction is ...

just like a hill climbing algorithm; they never know when an operation application improves the partial match. 

This kind of problem is also called a telephone pole problem for the telephone poles on the flat ground are similar to the peaks of the problem-solving performance.

Lessons learned:

We would be able to prove that GRAMY's construction procedure is complete wrt DSs.

If GRAMY is complete, then we would end up with having a cognitive model for effective construction.

There is no efficient algorithms with general construction exists -- pruning inappropriate constructions is not the key issue to make construction tractable. 

The key issue is the "revised GRAMY's specialized procedure," which says "for each partial match try to see if there exists a sequence of general construction operators to copy the DS configuration onto the problem figure."

Proof on the completeness of GRAMY

for each DS

for each goal

for each partial match

for each sequence of primitive construction operator that copies the DS configuration onto the problem figure that makes the DS have a perfect match

there exists an output from GRAMY that has the same perfect match

Cognitive task analysis of GRAMY

If we can prove the completeness of GRAMY just mentioned above, then we would be able to argue that GRAMY's construction procedure is what the students have to learn, which is the key for masterly learning.

The basic idea is that "pick up a useful DS and copy the configuration onto the problem figure."

The model of construction in GRAMY drops most of the operations my compass and straightedge.  The students still have to notice that they are using compass and straightedge when they draw lines with certain constraints. 

Next step

Prove that the revised GRAMY's specialized construction procedure actually works for a few DSs and a few partial matches.

We would be happy to find a pattern of those proofs. 

A step in a future toward an ITS

We do need heuristics of construction for GRAMY's construction procedure still produced too many constructions for students to deal with.
