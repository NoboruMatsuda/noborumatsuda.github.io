Claims on the construction procedure 
with specialized construction operators

 

Relationship between a partial match and a construction

How does a partial match guarantee that GRAMY doesn't draw a line that is not involved in a proof?

No way.  It is not a partial match, but a way constructions are made, that avoid irrelevant constructions.

Completeness of the construction operators

Using <direction, length> pair to categorize the types of constructions is good. -- They are clearly exhaustive.

Need to prove that for each category, we have a complete set of operators.

For <direction-know, length-unknown>, which is handled as an intersection of two rays, two constructions relating to perpendicular line is actually a special case of "having a bound point P1 on a normal line from the target endpoint and a bound ray passing through P1." 

 

Notes after the meeting

GRAMY can't find a construction that has the elements that don't appear in a proof.

So, we need to be careful what is the "completeness" for.
