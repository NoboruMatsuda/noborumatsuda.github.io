Review

Given a partial match, GRAMY constructs missing segments by the following steps:

  1. Nondeterministically pickup a missing segment (TS: target segment) with one of the endpoints bound
  2. Try all of the following steps to construct TS:
    2-1
    If both length (i.e., a congruent segment) and direction (i.e., a ray on the TS) of TS is known
    Then extend the ray and copy the segment
    2-2
    If direction of TS is known &
    there exists a third ray passing through the target end point &
    direction of the third ray is known
    Then plot the target end point as an intersection of those two rays
    2-3
    If length of TS is known
    Then try both of the following methods
    2-3-1
    If there exists a third segment sharing the target endpoint &
    its length is known
    Then plot the target endpoint as the intersection of two arcs
    2-3-2
    If there exists a third ray passing though the target endpoint &
    its direction is known
    Then plot the target endpoint as a point on the third ray away from bound endpoint by the length of the TS
  3. Go to step 1

"Completeness" of GRAMY

In general, there exist infinite many proofs that contain redundant constructions.  Since GRAMY's construction procedure is based on partial match, GRAMY can't find all those redundant proofs. 

GRAMY is complete in a sense that for all theorems that are satisfiable, it can find a shortest proof. 

Construction with a reference to a free ray

One must prove that the point constructed by the step 2-3-2 actually exists.  GRAMY avoid the proof by taking alternative construction: 

2-3-2'
If there exists a third ray passing through the target endpoint &
its direction is know
Then plot the target endpoint as a point on the third ray away from bound endpoint by the length of the TS
only
when		 there exists a free ray that is parallel or perpendicular to TS just constructed

The third segment appeared in 2-3-1 is not determined by its direction.  Hence, if there exists a free ray that is parallel or perpendicular to the third segment, then the target point is constructed by the same way as 2-3-2'.  

Not all the possible constructions are found...

With right-angle triangle-congruent axiom, GRAMY can find this construction by drawing a normal line from E to AD to plot Y and then plot X on AC such that AX = AE.  GRAMY can't, however, construct X by dropping a perpendicular from Y to AC.  This is because (1) Y is not a part of the partial match, and (2) GRAMY doesn't update the partial match on the fly as the construction proceeds. 

Intuitively, this doesn't hurt the completeness for most of the DS have several different sets of sufficient conditions.  It hasn't been proven, though.
