Things we need to investigate

Teaching proof writing as cognitive skills would fail, or less interesting.

c.f. Waff & Proof

Instead, investigating a relationship between proof writing and rest of mathematics would be more interesting.

We would teach students "what is proof for" instead of "what is proof."

Some researches on proof that don't focus on symbolic manipulation as cognitive skills

Tarski's world (Jon Barwise and John Etchemendy)

3D geometry quiz asking a relation between logical statements and a model.

Proof and refutations (Imre Lakatos)

Proof as explanations

Things to do next

See if the model tracing tutors (the Geometry Tutor and ANGLE) have answered one of these questions:

Can ITS teach students that proof is a chain of postulates from givens to a goal?

Can ITS teach students how to control their search?

If they didn't answer those questions, then that's what we would investigate.

Basically, I need to know (1) what does it mean to teach proof writing other than as cognitive skills and (2) if the model tracing tutors have addressed (or could achieve) these issues.
