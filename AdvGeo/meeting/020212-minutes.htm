Need to have my own theory in mind:

Then, go through the literature again to find supports and dis-supports

The theory-in-mind (i.e., our hypothesis on teaching/learning proof writing) must be valid in a sense they can be well operationalized.

We have GRAMY that is a sufficient and efficient model of theorem proving. 

Having GRAMY in mind would helps to build up a theory-in-mind.

 

From the literature survey, we would likely argue that proof-writing skill consists of the following factors:

+ definition of objects

+ postulates

+ parsing or matching diagram

+ search control / search strategy

- proof structure

and the question is how are they related each other.  

conjunctive: they are all related in a complex manner.

stepwise: they can be acquired one by one.  

 

What's DFA for?

For designing effective instructions.

For learning more about human learning.

Investigating a bunch of difficulties and observing how students would overcome those issues would end up with knowing more about how human learn.   

For example, we might see either implicit or explicit learning is good to learn search strategy.  

DFA would also lead us to an assessment issue:

What should be assessed and how. 

 

The correlation between VHL and proof writing:

1.  VHL and proof writing measured at the same time.

2.  VHL as a predictor of proof writing --  VHL at the beginning of a school year and proof-writing ability at the end.

 

See even if the students with high VHL would have low proof-writing abilities.

 

Need to clarify the students' difficulty in "language"? 

How does it differ from "basic factors," which are described in the language?

 

Reliability and validity of a study:

Be careful in the conclusions literature.  Even if they have data, different researchers might have different interpretations. 

Showing result is not enough:

We need a theory that explains a result.
