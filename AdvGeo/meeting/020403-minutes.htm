Questions we would want to answer with the data:

How students make (or the tutor provides as a hint on) FC and BC?

 

How does the tutor make contribution?

Possible coding in interests might be:

SPSS: suggesting to prove steps skipped.   Could be just "why?"

Subgoal: providing a next step when a student got stuck.

Justification: providing a justification for a proposition made by a student.

Correction: pointing out an error and correcting it. 

 

What is the nature of hinting?

What kind of hinting strategy would be effective?

The tutor might prioritize to provide sound hints. 

The second priority would be on making students understand the hints.

Tutor also try to teach a structure of problem solving by clarifying subgoal hierarchy. 

 

Can we read student's non-verbal backward reasoning off the order of propositions made?  What about tutor's hinting strategy?

One could make FC in any order, but if he/she is following BC, then particular subgoals must mentioned in particular order.

 

How often does the tutor make solicited hint (as a response to student's request or hedge) and unsolicited hints (as a tutor's spontaneous intervention).

Hint could be provided by:

students request,

students long pause, or

tutors whatever motivation.
