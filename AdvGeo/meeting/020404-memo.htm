Coding schema used in Andes

Local (focusing only on an utterance and its relatives)

Steps

1. Student performs correct step without help.

2. Student gets an impasse and repairs it.

A. Trigger of impasse-repair

- an error flagged by tutor

- an error caught by student

- a student's long pause

- a question / request from student

B. Help / Repair

* Content of a help could be either (1) step, (2) domain knowledge, (3) background, and/or (4) pure motivation. 

Help in Andes

- None (no help provided)

- Mini lesson

- Hint

- Bottom-out hint

Help by human tutor

- Mini lesson

- genuine question (disambiguation to understand student)

- comprehension question

- background question (not necessarily directly relating to the task; e.g., "Have you took a physics course?")

Other possible help

- Pointing a relevant object

- Providing information

- Assisting to set a goal

Repair (as a student's reaction)

- Repetition (just entering same equation over and over again)

- Character level search (search a particular pattern in an equation without considering its meaning)

- Wider search (a search with an expanded scope)

C. Result

- Advice taken

- Gave up

- Tables (postponed)

- by student

- by tutor

3. Undetected Error

4. Student elicits bottom-out hint with even trying to enter a step (i.e., S playing dumb).

5. Proactive help

- Tutor talks about a step before the student gets a chance to do it.

6. Everything else...

Global (considers a structure in dialog)

1. Sequence of steps

2. Goal hierarchy above each step

3. Tree that minimize constraint violation
