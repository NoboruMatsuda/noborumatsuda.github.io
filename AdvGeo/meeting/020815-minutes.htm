Forward hinting vs. backward hinting

when a student assert a proposition that is not justified, it might be reasonable to provide a hint on that unjustified assertion.  (backward hinting)

when a student complete a part of proof (i.e., assert everything in a sub-tree) and got stuck, the tutor could either provide a hint on what is derived from the sub-tree just completed (forward hinting) or provide a hint on top most goal by tabling the sub-tree (backward hinting).

Try to find both patter in the protocol data

 

Need to clarify some terminologies

assertion vs. supposition

A student could "assert" a proposition with a confident

A student could "suppose" a proposition with a hope

goal recognition vs. plan recognition

Within a single solution, the tutor must select a "goal" to work on next

Within a solution space, the tutor must select a "plan" (or a solution if you will) to work on.

The plan recognition may not matter, since a non-successful student doesn't have a plan at all.
