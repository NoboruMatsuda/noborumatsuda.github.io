Fading strategy:

There are two different degree of scaffolding that is the subject of fading:  the degree of grain size of problem-solving step, and the degree of abstractness of the message provided

Wadein (by Peter) might be a good example of fading scaffolding

When providing scaffolding with finer grain size (i.e., providing scaffolding on deeper steps), the tutor must mention a higher step.  Remember Catramborn's study on articulation of a goal structure.

How do you handle backup? 

The tutor might want to provide only the on-the-path hints. 

Hinting

Need to provide a "rational" hints by adapting the grain size of scaffolding.  This is done by assigning the tutor's estimation on a probability for the student having understood the corresponding problem-solving step.  How to maintain such estimation is quite questionable, though.
