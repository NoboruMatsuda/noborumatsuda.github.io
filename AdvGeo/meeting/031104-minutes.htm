Curriculum development: write a scenario for both AGT and control conditions

1.  Refreshment (or warm-up)

Provide basic geometry concept (i.e., definitions and postulates) excerpted from textbook

[Figure out concepts to be refreshed here] <- Things to do

2.  Demonstrate how to use the tutor

Pickup the first postulate to learn. 

Select a problem or two that can be proven with the selected postulate.

Snapshot (with highlighting elements of interest) and self-explanation would help students get familiar with the tutor (better than animation / video)

[Design a demonstration]

[Determine a sequence of postulates to learn]

[Also determine a sequence of problems to prove]

3.  Practical phase

Give 3-4 problems to practice the tutor

The end of practical phase might be signaled by fading student's hovering a mouse over GUI components

4.  Main tutoring session

Give a few more problems

5.  Assessment phase

Introduce new postulates and let the student prove problems that require new postulates

Design control condition

Control condition has the same structure of the session and the same sequence of postulates/problems, but slightly different version of tutor.  

[Determine what exactly the tutor in the control condition look like and also the demonstration in the 2nd phase.]

Evaluation of ITS

Measure the learning time in the assessment phase.  Our prediction is that the AGT group needs less time, because students would have learned operationalization.
