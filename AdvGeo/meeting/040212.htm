Research question:

Which instructional strategy is better to teach theorem proving with construction, forward or backward?

An answer for this question seems to be uncertain

If the Backward AGT wins, then it disagrees with a Anderson's finding that BC is very hard for students.

If the Forward AGT wins, then it supports Ken's conjecture that a major difficulty for students learning problem solving lies in operator acquisition (not search), which has yet to be tested.

Pre- and post-test

Must prepare two sets of tests.  Have a half of the subjects take one of them as a pre-test while another half takes the other one as a pre-test.  They then take the opposite test set as a post-test.

The test must consists of

Non-construction problems

Construction problems

One-shot postulate application problems

All but one justifications in a proof table is filled.  Students then identify which postulate must be applied to the empty justification. 

 

Warm up

The subject must be provided a warm up material (possibly a booklet showing postulates involved in the experiment and related geometry concepts).

The subject's mastery of the prerequisite knowledge (the postulates in the warm up booklet) must be tested

The one-shot postulate application problems in the pre-test can be used for this sake.

# of errors made before achieving mastery can be used as the pre-test score

 

Matched assignment is a must

Both conditions must have an equal competence in the pre-test and SAT scores
