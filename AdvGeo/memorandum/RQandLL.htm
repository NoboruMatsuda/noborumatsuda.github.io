Research Questions and Lessons Learned

Questions:

Is providing too much help spoil student's learning?

Degree of information provided

How is the "next-step" selected?

Fringe vs. non-fringe

What happed when a hint fails?

General-specific shift vs. goal-subgoal shift

What happen when a hint succeeds?

Spontaneous hinting

 

Lessons Learned:

Students hardly follow strict forward/backward reasoning.  They rather take opportunistic assertion. 

Human tutor almost always provides a hint on a single step for a request on next-step.

97% (87/90) of helps in 25 hint-sequences (for request on next-step, justification, and kick-off) can fit into the Cartesian-product categorization. 

Human tutor does not always provide hint on fringe. 

30% (3/10) of hint-sequences started at a fringe.

A backup strategy for a failed hint might be: to change a focus of hint from whole to specific, to change a target of hint by a step backward, to repeat the same hint.

A follow-up for a succeeded hint might be to provide a further hint on a step forward. 

Tutor may take an initiative of problem solving with spontaneous hinting over the multi-steps
