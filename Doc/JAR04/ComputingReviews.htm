GRAMY: a geometry theorem prover capable of construction
Matsuda N., Vanlehn K.  Journal of Automated Reasoning 32(1): 3-33, 2004. Type: Article
Date Reviewed: Sep 20 2004

This substantial research paper seeks a natural deduction system for Euclidean (plane) geometry that includes the ability to make constructions. Prior work in geometry theorem proving either did not include this capability, was not a natural deduction system, or was considerably more limited in the problem space for which it could find solutions. Because the authors seek to eventually embed their system in an intelligent tutoring system, it is also desirable that all possible proofs involving construction, not just one, are found, so that any solution proposed by a student user can be checked against the solution space.

Matsuda and Vanlehn review prior work, present earlier algorithms involving construction and discuss their limitations, present their own algorithm for finding proofs with operators for construction, prove it semi-complete, analyze its efficiency, and conclude with their future research agenda.

The paper is very well written, with Figure 3 alone deserving an award for crystallizing so much so well in a single, intricate diagram.

Reviewer:  Joseph S. Fulda Review #: CR130147 (0502-0251)
